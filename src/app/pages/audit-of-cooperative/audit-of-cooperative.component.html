Audit as Per Section 17 of the Co-operative Society Act, 1912 :

Apponting Qualification of Auditor

Chartered accountant, Government diploma in Co-operative Account

Appointment of Auditor

The appointment of an Auditor is done by Registrar of Co-operative Societies.

Rights of an Auditor

Auditor should check all the books, accounts, documents and securities of the society.

Books of Accounts and Other Records of the Society

Following books and accounts are prescribed by the State Government.

  • Cash Book
  • General Ledger
  • Stock register
  • Personal Ledger
  • Register of Members
  • Register of Shares and debentures
  • Minutes books of general body meeting and committee meetings
  • Property Register
  • Register recording loan applications
  • Maintenance of register of audit objections and their rectifications

Restriction on Co-operative Society

Let us now understand the restrictions that are imposed on co-operative society.

Restriction on Shareholding liabilities of the members of a society is limited, no member other than a registered society can hold more than 20% of the shares capital or shares of the society worth more than Rs.1,000/-

Restriction on Transfer of Share A member of registered society with unlimited liability cannot transfer any shares held by him or his interest in the capital of the society.

Restriction on Loan As per Section 29 of the Act, a registered society cannot give a advance or any loan to any person other than a member except with the prior permission of the Registrar.

Restriction on Borrowings A registered society can also receive deposits and loans from persons who are not members of the society and only such an extent and under such condition as may be prescribed in law.

Exemptions Central Government may exempt any registered societies or class of registered societies from Income Tax & Stamp duty or registration fees.

Reserve Fund, Contribution to Charitable Funds and Distribution of Profit

The first 25% of the net profit earned during the year can transferred to a Reserve Fund.

10% of Balance amount of net profit after transferring 25% to Reserve fund, a registered society can use for charitable purpose with the sanction of Registrar.

The balance amount of current profit and past years profit can be distributed to members of the society.

Dividend can be distributed according to rules and by-laws but cannot be more than 6.25%.

After special order of the State Government, society can distribute his unlimited liability profit otherwise not.
