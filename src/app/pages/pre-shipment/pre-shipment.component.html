Import Export in India is promoted and facilitated by the Directorate General of Foreign Trade under the Ministry of Commerce and Industry.

The DGFT issues the authorisation to exporters and monitors network of regional offices. The DGFT also implements the EXIM (FTP) Policy of India.

Import Export Polict means FTP is the policy that is very simple and transparent procedures which are easy to comply with and administer for efficient management of foreign trade in India.

The Policy designed for enhancing the country’s Import Export for economic fast growth and employment . The Customs Tariff Act and the GST Act are the other two important Acts which lay down how the Customs and GST duties shall be levied on trade, respectively.

Mostly India’s Import exportr is handled by its maritime transport. Jawaharlal Nehru Port Trust (JNPT) & Nhava Sheva in Maharashtra is India’s largest port, Currently, the country has a using inland container depots and freight stations for trade. Also developing other inland ports for Import Export.

For an Importer & exporters to avail incentives under the Foreign Trade Policy, it must first register with DGFT certification if IEC code as an EXIM Business.
